D3 Diffusion Tensor Imaging Data Visualization by Patrick Capps

Visualization 1

Here the DTI data is mapped to circle radii on a grid. Values range from 0 to 1, and are multiplied by 10 to get the circle radius.
Shape and color are uniform while size is used to represent value.

Visualization 2

This visualization instead uses color to represent the data instead of size. Rectangles of equal size on a grid are colored based on the data,
which is divided into ranges of 0.1 and ten colors are used, ranging generally from blue for low values to red for high values.
